A disability is a condition that limit us partially or completely depending on which type of disability it is. Around 1.3 billion experience significant disability. This represents 16% of the world's population, which is about 1 in 6 people. People with disabilities can die up to 20 years earlier than those without disabilities. They also have a higher risk of developing other health conditions. They also face lots of inequality due to the lack of accessible places as well as unfair conditions and discrimination.

Physical

What are they?

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), motor disability is "the sequelae or malformation that derives from a condition in the neuromuscular system at a central or peripheral level, resulting in alterations in the control of movement and posture."

Causes

There are multiple causes for a motor disability. However, when it comes to classifying this type of disability, they are categorized in physical as a disability condition.

Types

Motor disability encompasses many types of diagnoses and is very varied. Among the most common are:

  • Spinal cord injury
  • Chondrodystrophy
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Cerebral palsy
  • Spina bifida
  • Myopathy or progressive muscular dystrophy
  • Ataxia
  • Derived from head trauma

Cognitive

What are they?

People with intellectual disabilities have some limitations in functioning in their daily lives. They have a harder time learning social and intellectual skills to act in different situations.

Misconceptions about cognitive disabilities

  • Intellectual disability is not a mental illness.
  • People with intellectual disabilities are citizens like everyone else.
  • Each of these people has their own abilities, tastes, dreams, and needs.

Causes

Intellectual disability affects about 1% of the population. There are many causes of intellectual disability.

Treatment

The goal of treatment is to develop the person's full potential. Special training and education may begin as early as infancy.

Visual

What are they?

Visual impairment is considered to be a total or partial decrease in vision. It is measured by various parameters, such as near and far reading ability, visual field or visual acuity.

Causes

Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs when the body cannot regulate the amount of sugar in the blood. It can lead to serious health problems, including eye problems such as difficulty seeing, especially at night, sensitivity to light, and blindness.

Difficulties

Blind or partially sighted people are those who see nothing at all or have only a slight perception of light.
